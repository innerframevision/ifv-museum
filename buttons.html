INNER FRAME VISION

INNER FRAME CONSTRUCTS

THE FIRST EXHIBITION

The first exhibition presents the beginning of Inner Frame Constructs within the Inner Frame Vision movement.

In this direction, everyday objects become artistic frames that preserve entire worlds within themselves. They are no longer merely things, but independent visual spaces where memory, history, imagination, and architecture converge.

The exhibition is dedicated to a series of bone buttons inspired by Ancient Greece.

The exhibition introduces the Inner Button Frames series, in which the number of buttonholes defines the structure of each work:

One Hole — Miniature
Two Holes — Diptych
Three Holes — Triptych
Four Holes — Quadriptych
Five Holes — Pentarama
Six Holes — Hexarama

Each button is conceived as both a coin-like object and a frame. Its circular form becomes a symbolic entrance into another space. Beyond this boundary unfolds an inner world of classical architecture — temples, columns, courtyards, stairways, and fragments of an ancient civilization.

The button is no longer simply an object. It becomes a threshold.

The passage into the artwork begins not beyond the frame, but within the object itself.

As with museum artifacts, every bone surface bears its own traces of time. Differences in color, texture, and aging are preserved intentionally, allowing each piece to retain its individual history while remaining part of a single collection.

This exhibition marks the first exploration of how ordinary objects can transform into architectural frames and become entrances to entirely new realities.
